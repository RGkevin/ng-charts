IM UNSPSC COMPLETENESS

Of your item master records have a UNSPSC code at the 27% commodity level

Having your item master categorized to the United Nations Standard Products & Services Code (UNSPSC) at the commodity level can greatly improve your efforts in strategically analyzing spend and assist in your supply chain initiatives around standardization and cost reduction. Many organizations have also benefited by utilizing the UNSPSC to automate assignment of general ledger accounts and implementation of processes around formulary management.

IM MFG INFO COMPLETENESS

Of your item master records have a Populated Manufacturer Information (Name & Catalog Number)

Having the visibility into the manufacturers information for the products within your item master can greatly improve your processes around contract value maximization and compliance.

Complete Manufacturer information can also support your ability to conduct robust analytics around your spend and improve your speed to value when working on cost savings initiatives

Population of Manufacturer information in your item master can also support the timeliness of your response to product recall events within the market.

IM PKG CONVERSION COMPLETENESS

Of your item master records have complete packaging conversion factors populated

Having complete packaging conversion factors maintained and populated within your item master for the products you utilize can greatly improve the speed and quality of financial analysis for cost savings initiatives around product conversions. Having complete packaging conversion factors for the products within your item master also benefits procurement and inventory management by ensuring you are ordering and stocking adequate levels of products for your clinicians

IM HCPCS CODE COMPLETENESS

Of your item master records have a populated Level II HCPCS Code

It is best practice to maintain within your item master Level II HCPCS codes which supports the reimbursement process between your organization and Medicare / Medicaid as well as 3rd party payers. Though not likely consumed and utilized by supply chain, access to this information supports your revenue cycle team and can help support the improvement and optimization of revenue cycle processes - thus contributing to your organizations bottom line.

As you evaluate the completeness of your item master information understanding how this information ties to the utilization within your organization can assist you in better understanding the true impact to your organization. As you transact on these products lacking UNSPSC, Manufacturer Information, Packaging Conversion factors, and Level II HCPCS codes, you can understand better the systemic impact this can potentially have on your supply chain initiatives as well as the systems and business units who leverage this information. Tracking this performance overtime can help you measure the improvement (or lack there of) in addressing this data gap. As with any performance measurement, it is helpful to compare your performance to that of other health systems as well as those who are utilizing and operationalizing a data management solution like Novations.
